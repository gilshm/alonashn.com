Research

Soft robotic systems from materials to function

My research focuses on the material properties that determine the performance of high-performance dielectrics for artificial muscles. I also develop biodegradable materials that meet the electrical and mechanical requirements of soft, flexible, and strong robotic actuators.

A major part of my work connects these materials to complete systems, including wearable haptic feedback devices and assistive tools for tremor suppression. Before joining the Robotic Materials Department, I studied smart biocompatible polymers for biomedical applications, drug delivery, and biomedical adhesives.
